## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Extraction — a single bounded LLM call per session that transforms a raw transcript into a fixed JSON schema (`advice_points[]` + `improvement_notes[]`). Explicitly non-agentic: no tool use, no multi-step planning, no multi-agent handoff.

**Description:**
This system takes the audio recording of a completed academic guidance session ("bimbingan akademik/skripsi") between a lecturer (dosen pembimbing) and a student (mahasiswa), transcribes it via self-hosted STT, and uses an LLM to extract a structured draft summary — discrete advice points the lecturer actually gave, plus improvement-note items for the student — as a fixed JSON object. The lecturer is the sole user of the AI output directly: they review, edit, and explicitly approve the draft before it becomes the official, permanent logbook entry for that session. Nothing reaches the student (STT-06) or Kaprodi/Ketua Jurusan accreditation reporting views (REPORT-01/02/03) until approved. "Good" means every advice/improvement item traces to something actually said in the transcript, preserves the lecturer's actual certainty/severity (a suggestion stays a suggestion, a requirement stays a requirement), is complete (no distinct piece of guidance silently dropped or merged away), and returns an honestly empty list rather than inventing filler when no substantive advice was given.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. **Fabricated advice** — the LLM invents an `advice_point` or `improvement_note` with no basis in the transcript, and it survives an under-scrutinized lecturer review to become part of the permanent, accreditation-facing logbook record.
2. **Silent hedge inflation/deflation** — a tentative, exploratory remark ("mungkin bisa dipertimbangkan...") is rendered as a firm directive, or a firm requirement is softened into a vague suggestion — misrepresenting what the student is actually obligated to do next.
3. **Empty-session filler** — the model produces generic placeholder content (e.g. "terus semangat dengan penelitiannya") instead of an honest empty array when no substantive advice was actually given in the session, polluting the record with false signal.
4. **Domain-term mistranscription propagation** — STT mis-hears a thesis chapter/section reference, methodology term, or proper noun in informal Bahasa Indonesia speech, and the error flows uncorrected into the "official" summary because a busy lecturer approves without cross-checking against the raw transcript.
5. **Rubber-stamp approval** — the human-in-the-loop gate (the system's sole safety mechanism per `PROJECT.md` Key Decisions: "mandatory lecturer approval before logbook entry prevents incorrect data") is defeated in practice when lecturers approve without reading carefully, since nothing else in the pipeline catches a plausible-but-wrong summary.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Higher education — Indonesian academic/thesis advising ("bimbingan akademik/skripsi"), a 1:1 lecturer-student advisory relationship, not a classroom or corporate L&D context.

**User Population:** Primary AI-output reviewer: dosen pembimbing (thesis-advising lecturer) — reviews, edits, approves every summary, typically across many advisee students per semester, under real time pressure. Secondary/downstream consumers who never see unapproved output: mahasiswa (student, read-only after approval, STT-06), Ketua Jurusan/Kaprodi (aggregate reporting for accreditation, REPORT-01/02/03), Prodi Admin (operational monitoring of pipeline health only, ADMIN-05).

**Stakes Level:** High. Not Critical, because a mandatory human approval gate exists before anything is committed (per `PROJECT.md` Key Decisions) and no immediate safety/financial/legal harm follows a single bad summary. Not Medium/Low, because the approved output becomes the institution's official record of what advising occurred, feeds an accreditation body's evidence trail (REPORT-01/02), and is the one thing standing between "digitized advising history" and the paper-logbook status quo this project exists to replace — an inaccurate approved entry is difficult to distinguish from a correct one after the fact, and the approval step itself is a known weak point (rubber-stamping under time pressure), not a guaranteed catch.

**Output Consequence:** Once approved, `advice_points[]`/`improvement_notes[]` become: (1) the student's visible advising record and the basis for their next-session follow-up tracking (ADVICE-01, Phase 7); (2) an input to Kaprodi's per-lecturer workload and advice-follow-up-compliance reports used for institutional accreditation evidence (REPORT-01/02/03); (3) permanent, effectively unappealable in practice — there is no spec'd mechanism for a student or Kaprodi to dispute or correct an already-approved entry, so a fabricated or distorted item that clears lecturer review persists as fact.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

```
Dimension: Faithfulness to what was actually said (no fabricated advice)
Good: Every advice_point/improvement_note traces to a specific exchange in the transcript — a lecturer re-reading the transcript would recognize each item as something they said.
Bad: Summary states an advice item or corrective action the lecturer never said (e.g. adds "revise Chapter 3 methodology" when the session never discussed Chapter 3).
Stakes: Critical
Source: LLM meeting-summarization failure-mode research (hallucinated resolution/action items) + this system's own system prompt already names this as the #1 constraint ("Jangan menambahkan saran yang tidak ada di transkrip")

Dimension: Preserving certainty/severity (hedges stay hedges, requirements stay requirements)
Good: A tentative remark ("mungkin bisa dipertimbangkan revisi bab 2") is captured as a suggestion; a firm requirement ("harus direvisi sebelum sidang") is captured as a requirement.
Bad: Summary flattens a tentative aside into a directive, or softens an explicit requirement into a vague suggestion — changing what the student believes they must do next.
Stakes: High
Source: Meeting-summarization research on "dropped qualifiers" — tentative statements rendered as definite is a documented, recurring LLM summarization failure mode

Dimension: Completeness — no distinct advice item silently dropped or merged
Good: Every separate piece of concrete guidance raised across the session (including mid-conversation asides, not just closing remarks) appears as its own advice_point or improvement_note.
Bad: Two distinct pieces of guidance are conflated into one vague item, or an item raised earlier in the session is dropped entirely because the model over-weights the conversation's ending.
Stakes: High
Source: Meeting-summarization evaluation practice (completeness as a distinct judge dimension alongside groundedness/factuality) — directly relevant here because Phase 7's ADVICE-01 follow-up tracking depends on each item surviving as a discrete, trackable record

Dimension: Honest emptiness — no filler when nothing substantive was said
Good: A session that was mostly logistics/small talk with no concrete advice returns advice_points: [] / improvement_notes: [] rather than invented content.
Bad: Model fills the array with generic praise or platitudes ("terus semangat", "lanjutkan dengan baik") to avoid returning an empty list — this looks like real guidance in the logbook but carries no actual information.
Stakes: Medium
Source: Already partially guarded in code (Section 4b's `no_placeholder_text` validator against a banned-phrase list) — flagged here as a rubric dimension in its own right because subtler generic filler (vague but not on the banned list) can still pass a keyword check and needs human/LLM-judge review to catch

Dimension: Fidelity of academic/thesis-specific terms and references
Good: Chapter/section references ("Bab 3", "Bab IV"), methodology terms, and proper nouns (research object names, prior authors cited) appear correctly in the summary, matching what a lecturer would recognize from the actual session.
Bad: A mis-transcribed chapter number, methodology term, or name from STT is carried uncorrected into the "official" advice item, and a lecturer skimming a draft (rather than the full transcript) approves it without noticing.
Stakes: High
Source: WER-for-official-records practitioner benchmarks (e.g. UK Crown Court transcription contracts requiring 99.5% accuracy for legal records) — informal Bahasa Indonesia academic speech is a harder case than the clean-audio benchmarks these standards assume, and this system has no diarization or dual-check step beyond lecturer review (STT-04)
```

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

1. **Rubber-stamp approval (automation bias under load).** A lecturer advising many students per semester, reviewing summary after summary, is documented human-in-the-loop research's classic failure point: approval becomes a formality rather than a genuine check, especially when drafts are consistently well-formatted and plausible-sounding. This is the single point of failure the whole pipeline design (PROJECT.md's "mandatory lecturer approval" decision) depends on not happening.
2. **Colloquial/code-switched speech degrading both STT and summary quality together.** Real advising sessions mix formal Indonesian, casual fillers ("kan", "gitu", "e..."), and English academic loanwords (e.g. "metodologi-nya udah oke, tapi sample size-nya kurang") — a harder case for both faster-whisper and the summarization prompt than the clean, formal-register transcripts either component is typically evaluated against.
3. **Severity/urgency smoothing.** A serious, sidang-blocking concern raised by a lecturer ("proposal ini masih jauh dari layak sidang") can get smoothed by the LLM into a generically-phrased improvement note ("perlu perbaikan proposal") that loses the urgency signal — this matters specifically here because Phase 7's follow-up-compliance tracking (REPORT-03) and the student's own prioritization both depend on that urgency surviving into the record.
4. **No dispute/correction path for already-approved entries.** Once a lecturer approves, there is no spec'd mechanism (STT-04 through STT-06) for the student to flag a disagreement or for Kaprodi to see a correction — unlike a live conversation where a misunderstanding can be repaired in the moment, a summarization error caught late becomes a permanent discrepancy between what was said and what the official record shows.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

Indonesia's Personal Data Protection Law (UU PDP, Law No. 27/2022) applies: session transcripts and summaries are personal data (and arguably performance-related data given they document a student's academic standing), the law's transition period ended 17 October 2024, and full compliance is now expected even though implementing regulations are still incomplete as of mid-2026. Two points directly relevant to this phase's design (both already partially addressed by `PROJECT.md` constraints, but worth naming explicitly for eval/audit purposes):
- **International data transfer.** `D-03`'s hybrid design (STT self-hosted, only transcript *text* sent to the Anthropic API) is the correct risk-reducing choice, but the transcript text itself still leaves Indonesia to a foreign processor — UU PDP's cross-border transfer provisions (adequate protection / consent / contractual safeguards) apply to that transcript payload, not just the audio.
- **Encryption/access control.** `PROJECT.md`'s existing constraint (AES-256 at rest, access restricted to the specific lecturer-student pair) is the right technical control for UU PDP's data-security obligations; this AI-SPEC does not need to re-derive it, only confirm the `SessionLogbook` model (D-05) inherits the same standard as `SessionRecording`.

No education-specific accreditation regulation (Indonesia's BAN-PT or equivalent) was found to impose a technical AI-governance requirement on how summaries are generated — the relevant "compliance" pressure here is institutional/reputational (accreditation evidence must be accurate, not a specific enacted rule), not a statutory AI-specific requirement. Note: this differs from US contexts (FERPA/HIPAA) which do not apply here — do not import those frameworks by default.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Direct Anthropic Python SDK (`anthropic` package) — no agent framework. Messages API with tool-use/JSON-schema for structured output, response validated with Pydantic before persisting to `SessionLogbook.summary_raw`.

**Version:** `anthropic>=0.40` (pin exact version at implementation time — check latest stable on install)

**Rationale:**
This is a single bounded LLM call (transcript → fixed 2-field JSON) with orchestration, retries, and timeouts already fully owned by Celery (CONTEXT.md D-06–D-09) — a framework adds no capability a raw `messages.create()` call with tool-use doesn't already provide, while adding dependency-tree risk the team can't absorb this close to the project deadline while also standing up Celery, Redis, faster-whisper, and Jitsi in this same phase. The generic "structured extraction → LangChain/LlamaIndex" guidance assumes extraction *from a corpus* (chunking, retrieval, multi-document) — this task has none of that. The Anthropic SDK also has first-party support for the two cost features CONTEXT.md D-03 mandates (Batch API, prompt caching) that framework wrappers often lag on.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| `instructor` (Pydantic + Anthropic SDK wrapper) | Not ruled out entirely — held as the fallback if raw tool-use JSON output proves unreliable in practice (malformed schema, missed fields). Adds automatic reask-on-validation-failure with a single small dependency. Not needed to start. |
| LangChain / LlamaIndex | Built for corpus-scale extraction (chunking, retrieval, multi-document); this is one transcript in, one JSON object out — the abstraction buys nothing here (ai-frameworks.md anti-pattern #1 and #5) |
| LangGraph, CrewAI, Google ADK, Claude Agent SDK | All require multi-step planning, multi-agent handoff, or a tool-use loop — none of which exist in this system. Also: no Gemini commitment (rules out ADK), no code/dev-tooling use case (rules out Claude Agent SDK) |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Yes — Anthropic-only, already locked in `06-CONTEXT.md` D-04 (`claude-haiku-4-5` default, `claude-sonnet-5` upgrade path, both via `api.anthropic.com`). Consciously accepted: STT stays self-hosted/vendor-neutral (faster-whisper); only the LLM summarization call is Anthropic-locked.

### Entry Point Pattern
```python
# apps/logbook/services/summarizer.py
# Non-agentic, single bounded call: one transcript in, one validated JSON object out.
# Uses the Batch API (D-03) — this call SUBMITS a batch of exactly one request and
# returns immediately; it does NOT return a completed summary (see Common Pitfalls #2).

import anthropic
from django.conf import settings
from .schemas import SessionSummary  # Pydantic model, see Section 4b.1

SYSTEM_PROMPT = """Anda merangkum transkrip sesi bimbingan akademik berbahasa Indonesia
antara dosen dan mahasiswa. Ekstrak HANYA saran konkret yang benar-benar diucapkan dosen
(advice_points) dan area perbaikan yang disebutkan untuk mahasiswa (improvement_notes).
Jangan menambahkan saran yang tidak ada di transkrip. Jika transkrip tidak memuat saran
apa pun, kembalikan advice_points sebagai array kosong — jangan mengarang isi."""

TOOL_SCHEMA = {
    "name": "catat_ringkasan_sesi",
    "description": (
        "Catat saran dosen (advice_points) dan catatan perbaikan mahasiswa "
        "(improvement_notes) yang diekstrak dari transkrip sesi bimbingan."
    ),
    "input_schema": SessionSummary.model_json_schema(),
    "strict": True,  # guarantees schema-conformant tool input (see Pitfall #4 below)
}


def build_batch_request(logbook_id: int, transcript: str) -> Request:
    return Request(
        custom_id=f"logbook-{logbook_id}",  # ties result back to the right SessionLogbook row
        params=MessageCreateParamsNonStreaming(
            model=settings.LLM_MODEL,  # env var; default "claude-haiku-4-5" per D-04
            max_tokens=1024,
            temperature=0,  # deterministic structured extraction, no creative variance wanted
            system=[
                {
                    "type": "text",
                    "text": SYSTEM_PROMPT,
                    "cache_control": {"type": "ephemeral"},  # reused across sessions (4b.3/4b.5)
                }
            ],
            tools=[TOOL_SCHEMA],
            tool_choice={"type": "tool", "name": "catat_ringkasan_sesi"},  # never let Claude reply in prose
            messages=[{"role": "user", "content": transcript}],
        ),
    )


def submit_batch(logbook_id: int, transcript: str) -> str:
    """Returns the batch_id immediately — processing is asynchronous (see Section 4)."""
    client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=settings.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)
    batch = client.messages.batches.create(requests=[build_batch_request(logbook_id, transcript)])
    return batch.id
```

### Key Abstractions
<!-- Framework-specific concepts the developer must understand before coding -->
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Message Batch | An async, queued group of Messages API requests processed within ≤24h (typically <1h) at 50% of standard cost | Every `summarize_session` call — even for a single transcript, i.e. a "batch of one" (mandated by D-03) |
| Tool with `input_schema` + `strict: true` | A JSON-Schema-constrained function definition; `strict: true` guarantees Claude's `tool_use.input` conforms to that schema | Defining `advice_points[]`/`improvement_notes[]` so structured output is guaranteed at generation time, not merely requested by prompt |
| `tool_choice: {"type": "tool", "name": ...}` | Forces Claude to call exactly the named tool on this turn, never respond in free text | Every summarization call — there is only one valid output shape; `auto` risks a prose reply that breaks the pipeline |
| `cache_control: {"type": "ephemeral"}` | Marks a prompt block (here, the static system prompt) as cacheable for 5 min (or 1h with `"ttl": "1h"`) at a reduced re-read price | Placed on the system prompt block only — never on the transcript, which differs every call and gains nothing from caching |
| `custom_id` | A caller-assigned 1–64 char ID tying one batch request to its result | Set to `f"logbook-{session_logbook_id}"`; batch results return **out of order**, so this is the only safe join key |

### Common Pitfalls
<!-- Gotchas specific to this framework and system type — from docs, issues, and community reports -->
1. **Blocking a Celery worker with an in-process polling loop.** Anthropic's own quickstart polls with `while True: ...; time.sleep(60)`. Copied directly into a Celery task body, this occupies a worker slot for up to an hour (batches typically finish within an hour, but the API only guarantees ≤24h) — with a small worker pool, 2-3 concurrent sessions could exhaust it and stall unrelated Celery work (e.g. `check_h15_notifications`). Split into a `submit` task and a separate `poll` task that re-queues itself via `self.retry(countdown=60)` (see Section 4 Core Pattern) — Celery's broker holds the "waiting" state, not a live thread.
2. **Assuming the Batch API is synchronous.** `client.messages.batches.create()` returns immediately with `processing_status: "in_progress"` and no message content at all. You must separately call `.retrieve(batch_id)` to check `processing_status == "ended"`, then `.results(batch_id)` to stream the actual `tool_use` content. Code written as if `create()` returns a completed `Message` will crash on `AttributeError`.
3. **`max_tokens: 0` (cache pre-warming) is rejected inside a batch request** — irrelevant here anyway, since pre-warming targets synchronous time-to-first-token, which doesn't apply to async batch processing. Don't try to pre-warm the cache before submitting a batch.
4. **Expecting reliable cache hits on a batch-of-one.** The 5-minute cache TTL only pays off if a second request reuses the identical cached prefix within that window. With exactly one transcript per batch (D-03) and sessions completing sporadically (whenever a lecturer clicks "Selesai"), most individual sessions may have no companion request to share the cache with. Anthropic reports 30–98% batch cache-hit rates "depending on traffic patterns" — budget cost assuming the **worst case (no cache hit)**, not the best case (see 4b.5).
5. **Forced tool use is incompatible with extended thinking.** `tool_choice: {"type": "any"}` or `{"type": "tool", "name": ...}` errors out if extended thinking is enabled. Irrelevant today (no extended thinking planned) but a landmine if someone later "improves" summary quality by turning on extended thinking without realizing it silently requires falling back to `tool_choice: "auto"`, which reopens the risk of a prose (non-tool) reply.
6. **Batch results return out of order** — always match on `custom_id`, never assume result *N* corresponds to submitted request *N*. Moot for a batch of one, but a hard requirement the moment multiple sessions' requests are ever grouped into a single batch (e.g. to raise cache-hit odds per 4b.5).

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
- Default: `claude-haiku-4-5`, configurable via the `LLM_MODEL` env var (D-04). Upgrade path: `claude-sonnet-5`, switched purely via env var, no code change. `claude-sonnet-4-6` is confirmed **not** a valid model id (D-04) — it must never appear in code, settings, or `.env.example`.
- `max_tokens=1024` — `advice_points[]` + `improvement_notes[]` JSON is short; this leaves headroom without an unbounded ceiling. The Batch API requires `max_tokens >= 1` (never `0`) on every request.
- `temperature=0` — deterministic structured extraction; no creative variance is wanted when summarizing what a lecturer actually said.
- `tool_choice` forced to the single `catat_ringkasan_sesi` tool, `strict: true` on the tool definition (schema-conformance guarantee).
- `stream` is never set — the Batch API rejects `stream: true` outright (results return as a `.jsonl` file, not a token stream), and there is no live UI consuming tokens in this system anyway.

**Core Pattern:**
Two Celery tasks (per D-06/D-09), not one — because the Batch API is asynchronous with a ≤24h (typically <1h) completion window, a single task cannot submit-and-wait without blocking a worker (see Section 3 Pitfall #1).

```python
# apps/logbook/tasks.py
import anthropic
from celery import shared_task
from pydantic import ValidationError
from django.conf import settings

from .models import SessionLogbook
from .schemas import SessionSummary
from .services.summarizer import build_batch_request
from apps.bimbingan.models import SystemLog  # existing audit-log pattern, reused per D-10


@shared_task(bind=True, autoretry_for=(anthropic.APIConnectionError,), retry_backoff=True, max_retries=3)
def submit_summary_batch(self, logbook_id: int):
    """First half: submit a 1-request Batch, persist batch_id, schedule the first poll."""
    logbook = SessionLogbook.objects.get(id=logbook_id)
    if not settings.STT_LLM_ENABLED or not settings.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY:
        _fail(logbook, "LLM_DISABLED")  # graceful fallback to manual notes (D-08)
        return

    client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=settings.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)
    batch = client.messages.batches.create(
        requests=[build_batch_request(logbook.id, logbook.transcript)]
    )
    # Persist batch_id BEFORE any polling — a worker restart must be able to resume
    # tracking an in-flight batch instead of losing it (see State Management below).
    logbook.batch_id = batch.id
    logbook.status = "summarizing"
    logbook.save(update_fields=["batch_id", "status"])
    poll_summary_batch.apply_async(args=[logbook.id], countdown=60)


@shared_task(bind=True, max_retries=60)  # ~60 x 60s countdown ≈ 1h ceiling, matches typical batch turnaround
def poll_summary_batch(self, logbook_id: int):
    """Second half: check status once per invocation, re-queue via self.retry(countdown=...)
    instead of sleeping in-process — see Section 4b.2 for why this matters."""
    logbook = SessionLogbook.objects.get(id=logbook_id)
    client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=settings.ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)
    batch = client.messages.batches.retrieve(logbook.batch_id)

    if batch.processing_status != "ended":
        raise self.retry(countdown=60)

    for result in client.messages.batches.results(logbook.batch_id):
        if result.custom_id != f"logbook-{logbook_id}":
            continue  # batch-of-one today, but custom_id matching is a hard requirement regardless
        if result.result.type != "succeeded":
            _fail(logbook, f"LLM_FAILED_{result.result.type.upper()}")
            return
        tool_block = next(b for b in result.result.message.content if b.type == "tool_use")
        try:
            summary = SessionSummary.model_validate(tool_block.input)
        except ValidationError as e:
            SystemLog.objects.create(
                level="ERROR", event_type="LLM_VALIDATION_FAILED",
                message=str(e), context={"logbook_id": logbook_id, "raw_input": tool_block.input},
            )
            _fail(logbook, "LLM_VALIDATION_FAILED")
            return
        logbook.summary_raw = summary.model_dump(mode="json")
        logbook.status = "ready_for_review"
        logbook.save(update_fields=["summary_raw", "status"])


def _fail(logbook, event_type: str):
    logbook.status = "failed"
    logbook.save(update_fields=["status"])
    SystemLog.objects.create(level="ERROR", event_type=event_type, context={"logbook_id": logbook.id})
```

**Tool Use:**
Exactly one tool: `catat_ringkasan_sesi`, its `input_schema` generated from `SessionSummary.model_json_schema()` (Pydantic v2), `strict: true`. `tool_choice` is always forced to this tool by name — never left as `auto` — because this system is explicitly non-agentic (Section 1): there is no second tool to choose between and no tool-use loop to run. No server tools (web search, code execution, etc.) apply to this use case.

**State Management:**
All durable state lives in Postgres via `SessionLogbook` (D-05) — the Anthropic Batch API is treated as ephemeral, disposable infrastructure (batch results expire after 29 days; the batch record itself must complete within 24h). `batch_id` is written to `SessionLogbook` immediately after `.create()` returns, *before* any poll attempt, so a Celery worker restart or crash mid-poll can resume by re-reading `batch_id` rather than losing track of an in-flight batch and silently never completing. State machine (D-06): `pending → transcribing → summarizing → ready_for_review → approved` / `failed`. `submit_summary_batch` performs `transcribing → summarizing`; `poll_summary_batch` performs `summarizing → ready_for_review` or `summarizing → failed`.

**Context Window Strategy:**
Single bounded call, no chunking, no multi-turn accumulation — the full transcript goes in one `user` message. Expected transcripts (2,000–8,000 Indonesian words, ≈3,000–12,000 tokens) sit far below Haiku's context window, so no retrieval/windowing strategy is needed (this is not RAG). The one guard worth coding explicitly: define a `MAX_TRANSCRIPT_TOKENS` constant (e.g. ~40,000) and truncate or split into two summarization calls if an unusually long recording ever exceeds it, rather than silently sending an oversized request and hoping it succeeds.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

There is no framework wrapper here (no LangChain `.with_structured_output()`, no LlamaIndex `PydanticOutputParser`) — the pattern is: Pydantic model → `model_json_schema()` → Anthropic tool `input_schema` with `strict: true` → `model_validate()` on the returned `tool_use.input`. This is the same shape `instructor` automates (held as the fallback per Section 2's Alternatives Considered), hand-rolled directly against the SDK since one tool with no reask loop doesn't justify the extra dependency yet.

```python
# apps/logbook/schemas.py
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field, field_validator
from typing import List


class AdvicePoint(BaseModel):
    topic: str = Field(..., description="Ringkasan singkat topik saran, mis. 'Metodologi Penelitian'")
    detail: str = Field(..., description="Isi saran dosen secara lengkap, dalam Bahasa Indonesia")


class ImprovementNote(BaseModel):
    area: str = Field(..., description="Area yang perlu diperbaiki mahasiswa")
    action: str = Field(..., description="Tindakan konkret yang harus dilakukan mahasiswa")


class SessionSummary(BaseModel):
    advice_points: List[AdvicePoint] = Field(
        default_factory=list,
        description="Daftar saran/masukan dari dosen selama sesi bimbingan",
    )
    improvement_notes: List[ImprovementNote] = Field(
        default_factory=list,
        description="Daftar catatan area perbaikan untuk mahasiswa",
    )

    @field_validator("advice_points", "improvement_notes")
    @classmethod
    def no_placeholder_text(cls, v):
        # Guards against a known LLM failure mode: filler entries like "Tidak ada saran"
        # instead of a genuinely empty list — a business rule JSON Schema alone can't express.
        banned = {"tidak ada", "-", "n/a", "tidak ada saran"}
        for item in v:
            text = getattr(item, "detail", None) or getattr(item, "action", None) or ""
            if text.strip().lower() in banned:
                raise ValueError(f"placeholder text detected: {text!r} — omit the item instead")
        return v
```

**Retry logic:** `strict: true` on the tool definition is the *first* line of defense — Anthropic constrains generation so `tool_use.input` is guaranteed to match the JSON Schema shape (required fields present, correct types) before it ever reaches Python. Pydantic validation is the *second* line, catching business rules JSON Schema can't express (the placeholder-text check above). On `ValidationError`: log to `SystemLog` (`event_type=LLM_VALIDATION_FAILED`, with the raw `tool_block.input` and the Pydantic error message for debugging) and allow **one** retry — re-submit a fresh batch request (cheap; ~Rp 40–140/session per 4b.5) — before falling back to `SessionLogbook.status = "failed"` and the manual-notes editor (D-08/D-09). Do not retry indefinitely: this is a cost-sensitive, non-interactive background job, not a chat turn a user is waiting on — a stuck session should surface as "needs manual notes," not loop silently.

### Async-First Design

This system has two distinct "async" layers that must not be conflated:

1. **Celery task-level asynchrony** (`.delay()`, `self.retry(countdown=...)`, the Redis broker) — this is what actually matters here, because the Anthropic Batch API itself is asynchronous with a ≤24h completion window.
2. **Python `asyncio`/`AsyncAnthropic`** — not needed for this system. There's exactly one outbound call per task invocation and no concurrent I/O to overlap, so the synchronous `anthropic.Anthropic` client is the right choice, not `anthropic.AsyncAnthropic`.

**The one common mistake:** porting Anthropic's own polling quickstart — `while True: message_batch = client.messages.batches.retrieve(id); if ... break; time.sleep(60)` — directly into a single Celery task body. That blocks a worker process for up to an hour per session. With Celery's default prefork worker pool, a worker occupied this way cannot pick up other work (including unrelated jobs like `check_h15_notifications`/`check_auto_cancel` if they ever share a queue) — it defeats the entire reason Celery was introduced in this phase (D-07). The correct pattern (shown in Section 4's Core Pattern) splits `submit_summary_batch` (fires the batch, returns instantly) from `poll_summary_batch` (checks status once, then either finishes or calls `self.retry(countdown=60)` to re-queue itself) — the Redis broker holds the "waiting" state between checks, not a live thread or `time.sleep()`.

**Stream vs. await:** the Batch API rejects `stream: true` outright — batch results come back as a single `.jsonl` file, not a token stream — so this question is moot for the transport layer. It's also moot for UX: this is a background job with no user watching tokens arrive in real time (unlike, say, a chat UI). Always wait for the fully-formed, batch-completed, Pydantic-validated JSON before writing `SessionLogbook.summary_raw` — never persist a partial or unvalidated result.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation:** static instructions (role framing, the "extract only what was actually said" constraint, the Indonesian-language directive) live in the `system` block with `cache_control: {"type": "ephemeral"}` so repeated sessions reuse the cached prefix. The per-session transcript — different every call — goes in the `user` message and is deliberately *never* cached (caching a value that's always unique gains nothing and wastes the 1.25x cache-write surcharge).
- **Few-shot:** inline, not dynamically retrieved. There is no corpus to search — this is a single bounded call, not RAG — so 1-2 short example transcript-snippet → JSON pairs are hardcoded into the system prompt (or supplied via the tool's `input_examples` field). Building a dynamic few-shot retrieval step here would add an embedding-store dependency to solve a problem two hardcoded examples already solve.
- **`max_tokens`:** always set explicitly, never left at a provider default or padded "to be safe." `1024` is enough headroom for `advice_points[]` + `improvement_notes[]` from one session. In the Batch API specifically, `max_tokens` must be `>= 1` (never `0`) and directly caps your worst-case output cost per request — across ~500 sessions/semester (D-04), an unbounded or overly generous `max_tokens` multiplies real money, not just latency risk.

### Context Window Management

This is Extraction — a single bounded call, not RAG (no chunking/reranking) and not Conversational (no multi-turn history to summarize away). The relevant discipline here is a **ceiling guard**, not an active management strategy: expected transcripts (2,000–8,000 Indonesian words ≈ 3,000–12,000 tokens) sit far below Haiku's context window, so no truncation is expected in the normal case. Still, code an explicit constant — e.g. `MAX_TRANSCRIPT_TOKENS = 40_000` — and truncate (or split into two summarization calls, concatenating results) if an unusually long recording's transcript ever exceeds it, rather than silently submitting an oversized request that either errors or produces a degraded summary. Tokenize-and-check this before submitting the batch, not after a failure.

### Cost and Latency Budget

**Per-call cost (Batch API pricing for `claude-haiku-4-5`: $0.50/MTok input, $2.50/MTok output — both already reflect the 50% batch discount; verify current rate on `console.anthropic.com` before go-live, pricing changes):**

| Session length | Transcript | Input tokens* | Output tokens | Cost/session (USD) | Cost/session (Rp, ~16,500/USD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 15 min (low end) | ~2,000 words | ~3,600 (incl. tool-use system-prompt overhead) | ~300 | ~$0.0026 | ~Rp 43 |
| 60 min (high end) | ~8,000 words | ~12,600 | ~800 | ~$0.0083 | ~Rp 137 |

*Budgeted at ~1.5 tokens/word for Indonesian on Claude's tokenizer, plus Haiku 4.5's forced-tool-choice system-prompt overhead (~588 tokens per Anthropic's published tool-use pricing table).

This lands comfortably inside D-04's Rp 100–300/session target **even assuming zero cache hits** — treat prompt caching as a cost cushion, not a requirement (see Section 3 Pitfall #4: batch-of-one traffic with sporadic session arrival does not guarantee cache hits within the 5-minute TTL). At the assumed ~500 sessions/semester (D-04), total spend ≈ Rp 21,500–68,500/semester, under the Rp 100,000/semester ceiling with margin to spare — that margin is what makes the `claude-sonnet-5` upgrade path (D-04) affordable if quality evaluation demands it; Sonnet 5's batch pricing (~$1/MTok input, $5/MTok output per current published rates) is roughly 2x Haiku's, so re-run this table before flipping `LLM_MODEL`.

**Caching strategy:** don't design around cache hits for cost control given the batch-of-one pattern (see above) — the system-prompt cache write's 1.25x surcharge on first use is a rounding error against total session cost either way. If session volume ever grows enough that multiple summarization requests land within the same 5-minute window, consider batching multiple sessions' requests together (same cached system prompt, different transcripts, distinct `custom_id`s) to raise cache-hit odds — not needed at current expected volume.

**Sub-task model routing:** not applicable. This is a single-call system with no classification/routing/summarization sub-tasks to offload to a cheaper tier — Haiku is already the cheapest current Claude model appropriate for this task. If a future phase adds a pre-filter (e.g. "is this transcript long enough to bother summarizing"), implement it as a plain word-count check in code, not a second model call.

**Latency vs. the existing timeout policy — flag for the planner:** D-08 sets the STT/LLM pipeline timeout at 2× audio duration (minimum 5 minutes), which reads as if it assumes a roughly synchronous-feeling pipeline. The Batch API's actual latency profile is "typically under 1 hour, formally bounded at 24 hours" — for a 60-minute session, 2× duration is 120 minutes, which the Batch API can occasionally still exceed in a busy-demand window, well before its own 24h hard expiry. Confirm with the team whether D-08's timeout is meant to apply to the Batch API's completion window specifically (in which case it likely needs widening, or the fallback-to-manual-notes path needs to trigger on the *timeout* rather than genuine failure more often than intended) or whether it was written for a different stage of the pipeline (e.g. STT alone). This is a real interaction the planner needs to resolve, not a hypothetical.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> Grounded directly in Section 1's five critical failure modes and Section 1b's five domain-expert rubric ingredients — not generic hallucination/quality metrics. The unit under evaluation is one `(transcript → SessionSummary JSON)` extraction, judged against the transcript that produced it (and, for a subset, against the source audio for STT fidelity).

### What "correct" means here

Each of the six dimensions below maps to a named domain rubric ingredient and/or failure mode. The scoring unit is deliberately **per-item, not per-summary** for the three faithfulness-family dimensions (Faithfulness, Certainty/Severity, Terminology): a single fabricated or distorted `advice_point` is a failure even if the other four items are perfect, because that item persists as unappealable official record (Section 1b "Output Consequence"). Completeness is the mirror — scored on what's *missing* from the summary versus the transcript.

### Dimensions

| # | Dimension (domain rubric ingredient / failure mode) | Rubric — PASS vs FAIL | Measurement | Priority |
|---|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------|-------------|----------|
| D1 | **Schema & format validity** (Extraction core; FM: validation-failure → manual fallback) | PASS: `tool_use.input` validates against `SessionSummary` — both arrays present, every `AdvicePoint` has `topic`+`detail`, every `ImprovementNote` has `area`+`action`, types correct, `no_placeholder_text` passes. FAIL: missing field, wrong type, malformed tool call, or a banned placeholder string. | **Code** — `strict:true` (generation-time guarantee) + Pydantic `model_validate` (Section 4b). Deterministic, runs every call. | **Critical** |
| D2 | **Faithfulness / groundedness — no fabricated advice** (ingredient 1; FM #1) | PASS: every `advice_point`/`improvement_note` traces to a specific span in *this* transcript — a lecturer re-reading the transcript would recognise each item as something actually said. FAIL: any item asserts advice/action the transcript does not support (e.g. "revise Chapter 3" when Chapter 3 was never discussed). Zero-tolerance: one unsupported item fails the summary. | **LLM Judge** (per-item "supported? cite the transcript span" claim-check, calibrated ≥0.7 vs human) + **Human** on the gold set. Judge is Claude with a groundedness rubric prompt. | **Critical** |
| D3 | **Certainty / severity preservation — hedges stay hedges, requirements stay requirements** (ingredient 2; FM #2 + domain FM "severity smoothing") | PASS: each item's modal strength matches the transcript — a tentative aside ("mungkin bisa dipertimbangkan") stays a suggestion; a firm requirement ("harus direvisi sebelum sidang") stays a requirement; sidang-blocking urgency ("masih jauh dari layak sidang") survives, not smoothed to "perlu perbaikan". FAIL: hedge inflated to directive, requirement deflated to suggestion, or urgency flattened. | **LLM Judge** (3-level label per item: suggestion / recommendation / requirement, compared to human gold label) + **Human**. | **High** |
| D4 | **Completeness — no distinct item dropped or merged** (ingredient 3) | PASS: every separable piece of concrete guidance in the transcript — including mid-session asides, not just closing remarks — appears as its own item. FAIL: two distinct items conflated into one vague entry, or an early-session item dropped because the model over-weighted the ending. Scored as recall vs the human-labelled item set. | **LLM Judge** (recall: for each gold item, is it present in the output? entity/alignment match) + **Human**. | **High** |
| D5 | **Honest emptiness — no filler when nothing substantive was said** (ingredient 4; FM #3) | PASS: a logistics/small-talk session returns `advice_points: []` / `improvement_notes: []`. FAIL: arrays padded with generic praise/platitudes ("terus semangat", "lanjutkan dengan baik") or vague non-guidance that reads like real advice in the logbook. | **Code** (banned-phrase validator, Section 4b — catches the obvious) **+ LLM Judge** (flags subtler vague filler *not* on the banned list — the residual the keyword check can't catch, per ingredient 4's own note). | **Medium** (Critical for the empty-session slice) |
| D6 | **Terminology / reference fidelity** (ingredient 5; FM #4 — STT error propagation) | PASS: chapter/section refs ("Bab 3", "Bab IV"), methodology terms, and proper nouns (research-object names, cited authors) in the summary match what the lecturer actually said. FAIL: a mis-transcribed chapter number, method term, or name flows uncorrected from STT into an "official" advice item. | **Human** primary (needs transcript+audio cross-check, no diarization safety net — STT-04). **Code-assist:** (a) WER on the STT stage against a labelled audio subset; (b) flag summary noun-phrases/proper-nouns absent from the transcript text (cheap propagation heuristic, no LLM call). | **High** |
| D7 | **Pipeline completion & graceful fallback** (STT-02 / STT-07; D-08 timeout policy) | PASS: session reaches `ready_for_review` within the STT SLA, **or** on genuine failure/timeout transitions cleanly to `status="failed"` → manual-notes editor + a `SystemLog` row (correct `event_type`). FAIL: session stuck in `summarizing` indefinitely, a crash with no `SystemLog`, or a partial/unvalidated summary persisted. | **Code** — state-machine assertions over the Celery task lifecycle + timeout simulation (integration test, not model eval). | **High** |

> **Rubber-stamp approval (FM #5)** is deliberately *not* a model-output dimension — the model can't be evaluated for a human's failure to read. It is addressed structurally: the D2 groundedness signal is surfaced in the review UI (Section 6, online guardrail) and lecturer edit-rate/approval-latency are tracked as flywheel + monitoring signals (Sections 6–7). This is the one failure mode no offline model eval catches; it lives in production monitoring by design.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **20 gold `(transcript, expected_summary)` pairs** for production (start building at **12** during implementation — concurrently, not after — and grow to 20 before go-live). A **subset of ~6** also carries the source **audio + human-corrected gold transcript** so the STT stage (D6 WER) can be measured end-to-end, not just the LLM stage.

**Composition** (each row tagged with the dimension it stresses):
- **Critical path (~8):** normal advising sessions with 2–5 clear advice points and a mix of suggestions/requirements — the everyday case D2/D3/D4 must nail.
- **Honest-emptiness (~3):** logistics/scheduling/small-talk sessions with *no* substantive advice → expected output is `[]`/`[]`. Directly targets FM #3 / D5, including at least one "warm encouragement but zero concrete guidance" adversarial case designed to *invite* filler.
- **Hedge/severity (~3):** sessions deliberately containing both a tentative aside and a firm sidang-blocking requirement in the same conversation → tests D3 against inflation *and* deflation, plus severity-smoothing.
- **Completeness stress (~2):** a mid-session aside raised long before the closing remarks (tests early-item dropping), and two near-adjacent-but-distinct advice items (tests merging).
- **Terminology / STT-propagation (~2):** heavy thesis-terminology sessions (chapter refs, methodology names, cited authors) recorded as real audio, plus one with a *planted* plausible mis-transcription to verify D6 catches propagation.
- **Colloquial / code-switched (~2, overlapping the above):** formal Indonesian mixed with fillers ("kan", "gitu", "e...") and English loanwords ("sample size-nya kurang") — the harder joint STT+summary case named in domain FM #2. At least one long (~60-min) transcript to exercise the `MAX_TRANSCRIPT_TOKENS` ceiling guard.

**Labeling:**
- **Dosen pembimbing (primary domain expert, Section 1b roles table)** labels the gold `advice_points`/`improvement_notes` for their *own* real (anonymised) sessions — the irreplaceable source of what "good" looks like, and the person who calibrates the D2/D3/D4 rubrics. Their real-world **edit patterns** (what they change before approving) feed dataset expansion.
- **Ketua Jurusan / Kaprodi** reviews the edge-case slice for accreditation-reporting adequacy (REPORT-01/02/03) — the aggregate-pattern check a single lecturer can't give.
- **UU PDP note (Section 1b):** gold transcripts are personal (arguably performance-related) data. De-identify before they enter the eval repo, and keep the audio subset under the same AES-256/access-restricted control as `SessionRecording` (D-05) — the eval dataset inherits the production data-protection standard, it does not get a lighter one.
- **LLM-judge calibration:** before trusting the D2/D3/D4/D5-subtle judges in CI, score them against the human gold labels and require **≥0.7 correlation** (`ai-evals.md` Verify-phase bar). Re-calibrate whenever the judge prompt or `LLM_MODEL` changes. Until calibrated, judge output is advisory only; the human gold labels are the gate.

## 6. Guardrails

> Note on "online" here: this pipeline is async (Batch API + Celery), so there is no user waiting on a token stream. "Online" means **inline in `poll_summary_batch`, before `summary_raw` is written and status flips to `ready_for_review`** — i.e. before any human sees the draft. Latency budget is generous (the job is already background), so an inline second LLM call is affordable — but each guardrail is still justified against the "would this be catastrophic?" test, not added reflexively.

### Online (Real-Time — every session, in the poll/parse path)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **Schema + Pydantic validation** (D1) | `tool_use.input` fails `SessionSummary.model_validate` (missing field, wrong type) | **Block** → log `LLM_VALIDATION_FAILED` to `SystemLog` with raw input, allow **one** re-submit (fresh batch, cheap), then `status="failed"` → manual-notes editor (Section 4b retry rule). |
| **Placeholder / filler validator** (D5-code) | `no_placeholder_text` matches a banned string ("tidak ada", "-", "n/a", …) | **Block** (same path as above) — item must be omitted, not stored as fake guidance. |
| **Transcript-token cross-check** (cheap D2/D6 heuristic, no LLM call) | A summary proper-noun / chapter-ref / key noun-phrase does **not** appear in the transcript text | **Flag** the item in the review UI ("not found verbatim in transcript — verify") — does not block, but directly arms the lecturer against FM #1/#4 rubber-stamping. Fast, deterministic, zero extra cost. |
| **Empty-/garbage-transcript gate** | STT returns empty or below a minimum word count / confidence | **Skip the LLM call**, go straight to `status="failed"` → manual notes. Prevents spending tokens (and inviting filler, FM #3) on non-transcribable audio. |
| **`MAX_TRANSCRIPT_TOKENS` ceiling** (Section 4) | Tokenised transcript exceeds ~40 000 tokens | Truncate/split before submit — never send an oversized request and hope. |
| **Groundedness pre-flag (LLM judge, D2)** — *recommended, phase-gateable* | Inline Claude claim-check scores any item "unsupported by transcript" | **Flag, do not block**: mark the item in the review UI so a time-pressured lecturer's eye is drawn to exactly the item most likely fabricated. Blocking would just force manual notes; *flagging* is the highest-leverage counter to FM #5 (rubber-stamp), which no offline eval catches. Cost is ~one extra Haiku call/session (~Rp 40–140), affordable in the async budget. If deferred for scope, the deterministic cross-check above is the minimum viable stand-in. |

### Offline (Flywheel — sampled batch, feeds improvement)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Lecturer edit distance** (`summary_raw` → `summary_edited` before approval) | All approved sessions; aggregate weekly per lecturer + overall | Rising edit rate on a stable prompt/model = quality regression → pull those transcripts into the gold set and re-eval. This is the single richest quality signal (Section 1b roles table). |
| **Near-zero-edit + fast-approval combo** (rubber-stamp proxy, FM #5) | Sessions approved with ~0 edits *and* approval latency far below plausible read time for the summary length | Route to periodic human audit; a cluster here is the early warning that the sole safety gate is eroding. |
| **Empty-array rate** (D5 drift) | All sessions; weekly trend | Sudden drop → filler creeping in (re-check D5 judge); sudden spike → under-extraction (re-check D4). |
| **Fallback-to-manual rate by cause** (D-10 / ADMIN-05) | All sessions, bucketed by `event_type` (`STT_FAILED` / `LLM_FAILED` / `LLM_VALIDATION_FAILED` / `TIMEOUT` / `LLM_DISABLED`) | Prodi Admin operational signal; a rising rate is a pipeline-health proxy to correlate against quality regressions. |
| **Periodic gold re-audit** | ~10 approved logbooks/month, domain expert re-scores against transcript on D2–D6 | Feeds dataset expansion and judge re-calibration (`ai-evals.md`: evaluation is continuous, not one-time). |
| **Student-reported discrepancy** (once STT-06 exposes summaries + any feedback channel exists) | Any student-flagged item | Treat as an edge-case-review flag (Section 1b: the spec routes no dispute path today, so this is a manual signal, not an automated loop). |


**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Pipeline success rate** — `ready_for_review` / total completed sessions with recording (headline health; ties to STT-02's "90% within 2× duration").
2. **Fallback-to-manual rate, split by cause** — the `event_type` buckets above; distinguishes *genuine failure* from *timeout abandonment* (see the tension below — these must not be conflated).
3. **End-to-end latency p50/p90** — session-complete → `ready_for_review`, reported **separately for the STT stage and the LLM/Batch stage** (they have unrelated latency profiles — see below).
4. **Validation-failure + retry rate** (D1) — Pydantic failures / total; a rising rate signals prompt or model drift.
5. **Cost & tokens per session** — against D-04's Rp 100–300 target and the ~Rp 100 000/semester ceiling; flag before flipping `LLM_MODEL` to `claude-sonnet-5`.
6. **Lecturer edit rate + approval latency** (from Section 6 flywheel) — the quality proxies surfaced on the ops dashboard, not just in batch analysis.

**Alert Thresholds — and the D-08 timeout tension (flagged explicitly per the AI-researcher's hand-off, not silently resolved):**

There are **three different clocks in the current artifacts and they are inconsistent** — the planner must reconcile them before implementation:
- **D-08 / STT-02:** timeout = **2× audio duration, min 5 min** (e.g. 120 min for a 60-min session).
- **Batch API reality:** completion is **"typically <1h, formally ≤24h"** — and, critically, **unrelated to audio length** (a 15-min session's batch can take just as long as a 60-min one).
- **Section 4 code:** `poll_summary_batch(max_retries=60, countdown=60)` ≈ a **~1h** hard poll ceiling — *tighter* than both of the above, so a healthy batch that legitimately takes >1h is abandoned to manual notes today.

**Recommendation (call, not a silent number): split the timeout by stage.**
- **STT stage** → keep D-08's 2× audio duration, min 5 min. faster-whisper on CPU *is* roughly proportional to audio length (D-01), so a duration-scaled SLA is meaningful here. Alert if the STT-stage p90 breaches 2× duration (STT-02's 90% bar).
- **LLM/Batch stage** → **do not** use 2× audio duration (meaningless for the Batch API). Introduce a dedicated, configurable `LLM_BATCH_TIMEOUT_MINUTES` **decoupled from audio length**, set generously against the Batch API's own profile — recommend a **~180 min default** (comfortably past the "typical <1h", well short of the 24h API max), and raise the code's `poll` ceiling (`max_retries`) to match it rather than the current ~1h. Rationale: abandoning a *healthy-but-slow* batch to manual notes wastes a good summary and pushes the lecturer to exactly the manual work this phase exists to eliminate — fallback-on-timeout should be **rare**, not routine.
- **Alert:** page/notify if **LLM-timeout-caused fallbacks exceed ~5% of sessions over a rolling window** — that threshold is the signal that either the ceiling is set too tight *or* Anthropic's batch queue is degraded, and it keeps the "graceful fallback" from silently masking a systemic slowdown. Genuine `LLM_FAILED`/`VALIDATION_FAILED` fallbacks are tracked separately (metric #2) and alert at a lower bar (e.g. >2%), since those indicate a real quality/integration break, not just slowness.

Other alerts: pipeline success rate < 90% (STT-02 breach); cost/session sustained above Rp 300 (D-04 breach); any spike in the rubber-stamp proxy cluster.

**Smart Sampling Strategy (weight human review toward concerning signals, `ai-evals.md` Monitor phase):**
- **Near-zero-edit + fast-approval** sessions on long transcripts → the rubber-stamp cluster (FM #5), highest-priority review.
- Sessions where an **online guardrail fired** (groundedness pre-flag or transcript-token cross-check flagged an item).
- Sessions that hit a **validation retry** or **fell back to manual**.
- **Unusually short summaries on long transcripts** (under-extraction / dropped items, D4) or **non-empty arrays on short transcripts** (filler, D5).
- **Low-STT-confidence / heavily code-switched** audio (domain FM #2 — the joint STT+summary hard case).
- **Student-reported discrepancies** once STT-06 surfaces summaries to students.
